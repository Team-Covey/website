Behind the Broadcast

Operations

Every flight leg depends on tight planning, reliable systems, and smooth handovers across a full 24/7 schedule.

Core Areas

How the team runs the event

Operations is shared across dispatch, pilots, stream tech, and moderation so the event can stay stable and on-time.

01

Dispatch and Briefings

Route release, weather review, payload planning, and pre-flight checklists for each leg.

02

Crew Rotations

Pilot schedules and structured handovers to keep fatigue low and continuity high.

03

ATC and Network Flow

Coordination with online ATC networks and fallback plans when traffic or coverage changes.

04

Stream Reliability

Monitoring audio, overlays, ingest health, and backup scenes to prevent downtime.

05

Community Moderation

Live chat moderation, milestone callouts, and viewer support during high-traffic hours.

06

Donation Tracking

Transparent updates for campaign totals, sponsor activations, and on-stream targets.

Handover Standard

Leg transition checklist

Each crew swap follows the same pattern to protect stream continuity and flight safety.

Brief incoming crew: route status, fuel state, weather threats, alternates.
Confirm simulator state: controls, FMC entries, failures, and checkpoint save.
Sync broadcast team: crew names, segment timing, and any planned announcements.
Log and close: handover timestamp and notes for the next duty block.
